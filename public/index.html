Critical Next Step: Fund Your Trust

Your trust works properly only if your assets are transferred into it — this is called funding your trust. A trust that is never funded does not avoid probate.

Common assets to transfer include:

  • Real estate (your attorney will prepare a deed for your primary residence)
  • Bank and savings accounts — contact your institution to retitle in the trust's name
  • Investment and brokerage accounts
  • Life insurance — update beneficiary designations to name the trust
  • Business interests and partnership interests

Your attorney will discuss trust funding in detail at your signing appointment. Do not transfer assets until you have spoken with your attorney.

About Your Certificate of Trust

Your estate plan includes a Certificate of Trust — a short summary document that proves your trust exists and identifies the trustees, without disclosing the full details of your trust.

You will use the Certificate of Trust when you open trust bank accounts, transfer real estate, work with brokerages, and deal with any institution that needs to verify the trust. Keep several signed copies available.
